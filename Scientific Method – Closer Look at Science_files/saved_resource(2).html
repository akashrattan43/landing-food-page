What ISN'T TRUE about a hypothesis?

  • doesn't have to be testable

  • can be written in the "If......................then................"statement

  • it is an educated guess

  • can be proven true or false

When you say plants will grow BETTER, this ISN'T testable.  For example, if you have orange trees and they produce oranges, how would you determine which orange tree is GROWING BETTER?   You can't just stare at the plants and say, well the plant over here is growing better.  Why does BETTER mean?  What are you actually RECORDING between the two plants to determine which plant is actually growing better than the other?  Is it the # of oranges it is producing or it's height?  Those are testable!  You need to write one of those in your hypothesis instead of the word "BETTER."    This is a good hypothesis -->  Plants will PRODUCE MORE ORANGES if given fertilizer. <--   Now I know what I am recording/testing in lab.  I am recording the amount of oranges each plant produces.  So pick which one of these HYPOTHESES is a BAD hypothesis.

  • Dogs will learn tricks FASTER than cats.

  • Dogs are SMARTER than cats.

What would be a good hypothesis for our question, "do students who eat breakfast before a test, score higher than those who do not?"

  • If students eat breakfast before a test, then they will SCORE BETTER.

  • If students eat breakfast before a test, then they will SCORE WELL.

  • If students eat breakfast before a test, then they will SCORE HIGHER.
